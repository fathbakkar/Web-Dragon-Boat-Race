PACU JALUR

Heritage of the River, Pride of the State

Witness how culture, courage, and togetherness unite in the tradition of rowing that has transcended time. From the Kuantan River to the world.

More Than Just
a Boat Race

Pacu Jalur is a cultural heritage of the Kuantan Singingi community in Riau, Indonesia, that has thrived for hundreds of years. It is not just a rowing competition — it's a colossal display of teamwork, fighting spirit, and the identity of the villages along the Batang Kuantan River. With dozens of paddlers moving in unison, each stroke is a symbol of community strength.

Each jalur (boat) is crafted from selected solid wood, beautifully decorated, and given a distinctive name that carries hope and prayers. Behind each jalur, there are stories of the village, the hard work of artisans, and months of training.
